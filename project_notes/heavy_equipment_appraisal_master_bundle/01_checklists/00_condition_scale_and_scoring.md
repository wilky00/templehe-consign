# Condition Scale and Scoring Guide

## Condition Scale

- **5 - Excellent**: Low-hour or exceptionally maintained unit. Clean, fully functional, strong cosmetics, no material leaks, no immediate repairs expected.
- **4 - Good**: Normal used condition for age/hours. Fully operational with minor wear, minor cosmetic flaws, and no major immediate repair needed.
- **3 - Fair**: Operational but shows meaningful wear. Multiple defects or deferred maintenance present. Marketability reduced and buyer likely expects repairs.
- **2 - Poor**: Runs or partially functions but has major defects, structural concerns, heavy leakage, significant wear, or failed systems. Strong value discount.
- **1 - Inoperable**: Non-running or unable to demonstrate critical functions. Salvage, parts, or major project unit unless repaired.
- **0 - Not Verified**: Tech could not inspect or confirm. Use sparingly and explain why.

## Scoring Rules
- Score each major component from 0 to 5 using the shared definitions above.
- Use `0 - Not Verified` only when the tech truly could not inspect or confirm the item.
- Use category weights to calculate the weighted overall condition score.
- Record red flags separately. A machine can score reasonably well but still require management review if red flags are present.


## Suggested Marketability Scale
- **Fast sell**: broad buyer appeal, limited repair exposure, clean photos, no major red flags
- **Average**: normal time to sell for age/hours/category
- **Slow sell**: niche demand, repair exposure, rough cosmetics, or weak attachment package
- **Salvage risk**: non-running, structural issues, missing major components, or title/ownership problems
