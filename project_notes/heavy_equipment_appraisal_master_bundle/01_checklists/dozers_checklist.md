# Dozers Appraisal Checklist

Complete the shared intake first, then use this category checklist.

## Quick ID / Configuration

- Open station / canopy / enclosed cab
- 2WD / 4WD / track style if applicable
- Emissions label visible / not visible
- Running / partially running / non-running
- Hours displayed and whether believed accurate

## Major Components to Score (0 to 5)

- Engine
- Transmission/steering
- Final drives
- Undercarriage
- Blade assembly
- Ripper assembly
- Hydraulic system
- Cab/controls

## Inspection Prompts

- Cold start
- Blow-by
- Undercarriage wear
- Final drive noise/leaks
- Blade push arm wear
- Trunnion wear
- Ripper frame wear
- Steering response
- Hydraulic leaks
- Cab/AC condition

## Common Attachments / Options to Capture

- PAT blade
- SU blade
- U blade
- Winch
- Single shank ripper
- Multi-shank ripper
- Sweeps
- Rear screen

## Required Photos

- 4 corner exterior
- Serial plate
- Hour meter
- Cab interior
- Engine compartment
- Both track sides
- Sprockets/idlers/rollers
- Blade cutting edge
- Ripper
- Visible leaks/damage

## Key Value Drivers

- Undercarriage remaining life
- Hours
- Enclosed cab vs open canopy
- PAT vs straight blade
- Ripper equipped
- Steering/final drive condition
- Emission tier
- Service records
- Major rebuilds
- Transport width issues if purchased

## Red Flags

- Structural cracks, poor weld repairs, or missing guards
- Major active hydraulic, engine, coolant, or drivetrain leaks
- Meter inconsistency or suspected hour tampering
- Serial plate missing or unreadable
- Safety systems inoperative
- Major component failure, non-running status, or inability to demonstrate core functions
- Attachment mismatch, missing coupler parts, or significant bucket/blade/drum/body wear

## Appraiser Notes
- Cosmetics summary
- Immediate repair needs
- Resale readiness
- Consignment suitability
- Direct-buy suitability
